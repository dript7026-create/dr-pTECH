# Bango-Patoot: Underhive Nocturne

## Premise
Bango is an autism-spectrum anthropomorphic Horned-Urs, descended from a prehistoric line of horned, spined, feather-scaled bears who survive in the forgotten service tunnels beneath a steam-haunted city. Patoot, his hybrid reptile-dodo-ostrid companion, serves as scout, ward, mount-assist, and aerial spell focus. When Bango's sister Tula is taken by a cyberwitch cult that feeds on hive-memory, industrial runoff, and stolen devotion, the pair descend through alleys, shrines, maintenance vaults, sewer-catacombs, condemned theaters, apocryphal chapels, and machine-grottos to break a city-scale ritual engine.

## Plot Shape
The game condenses the broad appeal of multiple duo-platformer adventures into one original storyline:
- Act 1: Tula is taken during a hive-feast blackout and Bango/Patoot follow the signs into the urban underbelly.
- Act 2: What begins as a kidnapping reveals a citywide convergence of gangs, cults, machine-monks, sewer cryptids, and engineered blasphemies all orbiting the cyberwitch's ascension engine.
- Act 3: The pair restore the apiary shrine network, master hybrid feral-magic and salvage-tech disciplines, and climb back upward through the city core to rupture the ritual lattice.

## World Structure
- One central 3D hubworld.
- Seven major subworlds branching off the hub.
- Every subworld contains seamless interior/exterior traversal pockets instead of hard-separated micro rooms where practical.
- Hub and levels preserve a clean read similar to classic 3D platformers while becoming denser, darker, and more systemic.
- Enterable structures should feel naturally embedded in the same continuous city-body.

## Subworld Biomes
- Brassroot Borough: a rusted worker district wrapped around abandoned apiaries.
- Saint Voltage Arcade: a neon chapel-market where gangs and miracle vendors overlap.
- Ossuary Transit: broken rail tunnels inhabited by shrine thieves and bone-machines.
- Gutterwake Sewers: cryptid nests, floodgates, fungus altars, and drowned workshops.
- Cinder Tenements: stacked apartments with cult shrines hidden behind ordinary rooms.
- Aviary of the Last Broadcast: signal towers, giant cages, and haunted public-address systems.
- The Witchcoil Spire: the cyberwitch's vertical ritual-factory and final ascent.

## Core Gameplay Loops
### Exploration Loop
- Enter hub.
- Unlock movement gates with learned traversal skills.
- Enter a subworld.
- Route between puzzles, combat arenas, shrines, rescue NPCs, hidden structures, and macro collectibles.
- Return to hub or next level with new keys and growth resources.

### Collectathon Loop
- Find Hive Sigils as major progression tokens.
- Collect Nocturne Notes as score/resource currency.
- Rescue Lantern Kin as set-completion bonuses.
- Use all three loops to unlock world gates, shrine upgrades, and build branches.

### Shrine Loop
- Reach an apiary shrine.
- Refine gathered honey into stat points and skill catalysts.
- Rest, recover, re-spec limited branches, and re-route fast travel.
- Trigger world-state changes and NPE relationship updates.
- Shrines operate as sanctified apiary reliquaries hidden inside the city's plague of folklore and machinery.

### Combat Loop
- Real-time melee, ranged, magic, and traversal-combat interplay.
- Stamina budgets light and heavy strings, air actions, dodge options, ward stances, and spell casts.
- Enemy ecology scales with player history, shrine choices, and EgoSphere-tracked relationship memory.
- Horror pressure comes from density, threat layering, and world-state escalation rather than pure gore.

## Move Roster Strategy
- Target content scale: 1000 move slots.
- Not 1000 bespoke animations from day one.
- Moves are generated from stance, locomotion mode, body-part emphasis, elemental modifier, combo index, utility tags, and fear-state/horror-context modifiers.
- Each move stores 3 to 6 authored keyframes plus inbetween hints.
- Runtime/authoring tools can expand those into full animation curves later.

## NPE Relationship System
- Built on EgoSphere.
- Tracks affinity, fear, trust, mimicry, rivalry, and memory of player behavior.
- Affects side content, vendor behavior, rescue responses, world-state changes, cult hostility, gang territory mood, and combat assistance.

## Completion Targets
- Standard completion target: 17 hours for a new player.
- 100% completion target: 23 hours.
- These targets assume approachable but layered platforming, optional mastery content, and readable build systems.

## 3DS Presentation Strategy
- Top screen: stereoscopic 3D world render plus HUD overlay.
- Bottom screen: map, shrine tree, party/move pages, and relationship summaries.
- Hybrid presentation: 2D authored sheets mapped to 3D billboards, skeletal rigs, lightweight environment meshes, and underworld parallax layers.

## Legal/Creative Constraint
- This game should remain unmistakably original in naming, story, silhouette language, enemy design, worldbuilding, and collectible terminology even while it draws from the general appeal of mascot-platformer exploration and duo-character traversal design.
