Bango-Patoot: Underhive Nocturne

Document Purpose
This is the full design brief for the original Nintendo 3DS project Bango-Patoot. It consolidates story, world structure, progression rules, system logic, and a full production asset list for later implementation.

High Concept
Bango-Patoot is an original 3D collectathon action-RPG with horror-fantasy worldbuilding, shrine-based progression, and duo traversal. The player controls Bango, a horned prehistoric bear-descended undercity survivor, and Patoot, a bird-reptile hybrid companion who extends movement, combat, and puzzle expression. The game unfolds through a vertical city-underworld where industrial blasphemy, hive-mysticism, gang rule, and cryptid folklore overlap.

Core Pillars
1. Duo traversal in dense readable 3D spaces.
2. Collectathon progression fused with an RPG attribute and skill system.
3. Urban horror expressed through atmosphere, folklore, architecture, and social decay rather than gore alone.
4. Shrine-centered build refinement, route planning, and social-state shifts.
5. Original silhouettes, terms, factions, and narrative identity.

Product Definition
- Genre: 3D collectathon action-RPG.
- Platform target: Nintendo 3DS.
- Camera: third-person free camera with soft lock in combat zones.
- Session shape: 20 to 45 minute exploration runs between shrine rest points.
- Target playtime: 17 hours main path, 23 hours full completion.
- Rating target: Teen.

Player Fantasy
The player should feel like an odd but gifted survivor descending through a city-body that behaves like a haunted organism. Bango is physically grounded, stubborn, sensory-attuned, and resilient. Patoot makes movement and situational awareness feel clever rather than merely fast. Together they are feral, ritual, practical, emotionally distinct, and visibly inseparable.

Narrative Overview
Premise:
During the annual Nightcomb Feast, the lights of Brassroot Borough fail in a cascading blackout called a hushfall. In the darkness, the cyberwitch Matra Vey steals Tula, Bango's sister, and several hive-kin children, using hymn-engines to siphon memory, devotion, and fear from the undercity. Bango and Patoot begin a rescue mission that expands into a larger revelation: the city is being prepared as a living ritual machine.

Themes:
- Family obligation versus self-erasure.
- Disability-aware perception as strength.
- Ritual versus institution.
- Folklore surviving inside infrastructure.
- Companionship as adaptive intelligence.
- Memory as a civic resource.

Main Cast
Bango:
Autism-spectrum Horned-Urs descended from prehistoric tunnel-dwellers. Literal, pattern-focused, protective, and physically powerful. Learns that his precise perception is the only thing that can consistently detect the city's false ritual harmonics.

Patoot:
Bird-reptile hybrid companion. Functions as scout, traversal assist, ritual familiar, and emotional counterpoint.

Tula:
Bango's older sister and hive-keeper apprentice. Her stable chant-memory makes her vital to Matra Vey's plan.

Mother Comb Edda:
First shrine guide and apiary elder.

Vilm Siltcoat:
Salvage broker and practical ally.

Sister Halceon:
Defected chapel enforcer who reveals doctrinal truth about the machine-cults.

Matra Vey:
Cyberwitch antagonist who wants to convert the entire city into a controlled thinking body.

Full Narrative Structure
Prologue: The Hushfall
The player learns basic movement during the Nightcomb Feast. A blackout raid steals Tula and several children. Bango reaches the first shrine too late and receives the first echo-memory from Tula.

Act I: Brassroot Borough
Restore the first shrine circuit, learn the economy, and hunt the kidnappers' route. End boss: Honey Debt Collector.

Act II: Saint Voltage Arcade
Infiltrate chapel-market circuits and expose the devotion-to-machine pipeline. End boss: Bishop Static.

Act III: Ossuary Transit
Reopen the rail backbone and discover how the city buried the unwanted inside infrastructure. End boss: Railmouth Ossivar.

Act IV: Gutterwake Sewers
Interrupt the alchemical runoff that feeds the ritual engine. End boss: Maw-of-Silt.

Act V: Cinder Tenements
Break the apartment chapel network and rescue captive households. End boss: Caretaker Red Mother.

Act VI: Aviary of the Last Broadcast
Seize the last clean signal tower and prepare Tula's counter-hymn. End boss: Broadcast Harrower.

Act VII: Witchcoil Spire
Rescue Tula, ascend the ritual-factory, and destroy Matra Vey's obedience lattice.

Ending
The city survives but remains scarred. The shrines are restored as voluntary civic memory points rather than control nodes.

Postgame
Unlocks sealed shrines, remixed districts, faction-resolution quests, and an optional true ending route.

World Structure
Hub:
Apiary Junction, the central undercity plaza connected to all seven major districts.

District Order:
1. Brassroot Borough
2. Saint Voltage Arcade
3. Ossuary Transit
4. Gutterwake Sewers
5. Cinder Tenements
6. Aviary of the Last Broadcast
7. Witchcoil Spire

District Rules
- Each district has 1 major shrine, 2 minor shrines, 1 boss arena, 3 to 5 traversal landmarks, 2 rescue chains, and 1 faction side route.
- Each district visibly changes after purification.

Gameplay Systems
Traversal:
Bango provides run, jump, ledge grab, push, pull, slam, horn bash, and sprint. Patoot adds hover assist, scouting, switch pecking, line carries, and midair correction. Upgrades add wall-runs, honey rail grinding, shrine pull grapples, and combat traversal cancels.

Collectibles:
- Hive Sigils: macro progression.
- Nocturne Notes: currency.
- Lantern Kin: rescue set bonuses.
- Shrine Pollen: upgrade component.
- Choir Scraps: lore and hidden route keys.

Attributes:
- Vigor
- Honeyheart
- Taloncraft
- Nerve
- Grit
- Arcana

Stamina Logic:
- Base stamina: 100.
- Light actions: 8 to 14 cost.
- Heavy actions: 20 to 35 cost.
- Dodge: 18 cost.
- Aerial correction: 10 cost.

Move Catalog Logic:
Moves are composed from stance, locomotion mode, body emphasis, angle, element or ward modifier, combo index, utility tags, and fear-state modifier. Each move stores stamina cost, hit count, damage scalar, knockback scalar, keyframe count, interpolation hints, and sound profile.

Skill Trees:
- Horn Path
- Claw Path
- Patoot Path
- Shrine Path
- Salvage Path
- Resonance Path

Shrines:
Shrines are checkpoints, heal points, build refinement points, fast travel anchors, and district-state triggers.

Quest Logic:
- Main quest for district completion and Tula recovery.
- Rescue chains for Lantern Kin.
- Faction quests for gangs, shrine keepers, salvagers, and chapel defectors.
- Myth quests for hidden bosses and codex pages.

Relationship Logic:
NPEs track trust, fear, debt, respect, rivalry, and mimicry. These values alter prices, warnings, ambush frequency, pursuit persistence, rescue access, and district social response.

Save Data
Stores district completion, shrine states, collectibles, builds, move unlocks, relationship values, quest flags, and currencies.

Presentation
Top Screen:
- stereoscopic world view
- combat and objective HUD

Bottom Screen:
- map
- shrine menu
- skill pages
- move pages
- relationship summaries

Audio Direction
Industrial drone, ritual percussion, broken choir, reed textures, and hive-like harmonics. Districts need clear sonic identities. Shrine audio must sound restorative but not soft.

Art Direction
Horned-prehistoric anatomy meets soot-blackened infrastructure, brass devotional machinery, old urban folklore, and hive geometry. Colors lean toward ochre, rust, soot black, dead teal, lantern amber, acidic green, and shrine gold.
