# Bango-Patoot: Underhive Nocturne

## Document Purpose
This document is the full game design brief for the original Nintendo 3DS project Bango-Patoot. It consolidates story, world structure, progression logic, systemic rules, production requirements, and a practical asset bible for later implementation.

## High Concept
Bango-Patoot is an original 3D collectathon action-RPG with horror-fantasy worldbuilding, shrine-based progression, and duo-character traversal. The player controls Bango, a horned prehistoric bear-descended undercity survivor, and Patoot, a bird-reptile hybrid companion who extends movement, combat, and puzzle expression. The game unfolds through a vertical city-underworld where industrial blasphemy, hive-mysticism, gang rule, and cryptid folklore overlap.

## Core Pillars
1. Duo traversal in dense readable 3D spaces.
2. Collectathon progression fused with an RPG attribute and skill system.
3. Urban horror expressed through atmosphere, folklore, architecture, and social decay rather than gore alone.
4. Shrine-centered build refinement, route planning, and social-state shifts.
5. Original silhouettes, terms, factions, and narrative identity.

## Product Definition
- Genre: 3D collectathon action-RPG.
- Platform target: Nintendo 3DS.
- Camera: third-person free camera with soft lock in combat zones.
- Session shape: 20 to 45 minute exploration runs between shrine rest points.
- Target playtime: 17 hours main path, 23 hours full completion.
- Rating target: Teen.

## Player Fantasy
The player should feel like an odd but gifted survivor descending through a city-body that behaves like a haunted organism. Bango is physically grounded, stubborn, sensory-attuned, and resilient. Patoot makes movement and situational awareness feel clever rather than merely fast. Together they are not a joke pair, nor a grimdark soldier pair. They are feral, ritual, practical, emotionally distinct, and visibly inseparable.

## Narrative Overview

### Premise
During the annual Nightcomb Feast, the lights of Brassroot Borough fail in a cascading blackout known locally as a hushfall. In the darkness, the cyberwitch Matra Vey steals Tula, Bango's sister, and several hive-kin children, using surgically tuned hymn-engines to siphon memory, devotion, and fear from the undercity. Bango and Patoot begin a rescue mission that expands into a larger revelation: the city itself is being prepared as a living ritual machine. If Matra Vey ascends through the Witchcoil Spire and binds the hive-memory lattices to the city's rail, power, and broadcast arteries, every district will become part of a permanent obedience engine.

### Themes
- Family obligation versus self-erasure.
- Disability-aware perception as strength, not tragedy.
- Ritual versus institution.
- Folklore surviving inside infrastructure.
- Companionship as adaptive intelligence.
- Memory as a civic resource that can be protected or industrialized.

### Main Cast

#### Bango
An autism-spectrum Horned-Urs descended from prehistoric tunnel-dwellers. Bango is literal, pattern-focused, protective, and physically powerful without being conventionally heroic. He notices inconsistencies in sound, light rhythm, and architecture faster than most other characters. His arc is about learning that his precise way of reading the world is not a deficit but the one perception mode able to detect the city's false ritual harmonics.

#### Patoot
A sharp-eyed reptile-dodo-ostrid companion raised with Bango since infancy. Patoot is both mount-assist and ritual familiar. Patoot reads vertical spaces, incoming threats, and emotional temperature long before Bango does. Patoot's behavior system should feel like affectionate problem-solving, not comic relief.

#### Tula
Bango's older sister, a hive-keeper apprentice whose chant-memory is unusually stable. Tula's capture is the inciting event, but she remains an active presence through shrine echoes, intercepted prayers, and eventual remote sabotage of the ritual lattice from within captivity.

#### Mother Comb Edda
Keeper of the first apiary reliquary. She functions as the shrine tutorial voice and moral compass, but she is not naive. She understands that every restored shrine will also alert rival factions.

#### Vilm Siltcoat
Salvage broker and reluctant ally. Vilm opens the gear economy, marks hidden maintenance paths, and represents the practical middle layer between gangs and shrine-keepers.

#### Sister Halceon
A former chapel enforcer turned penitent guide. She provides doctrinal context for the machine-cults and optional side quests tied to confession booths, condemned reliquaries, and recovered hymn tape.

#### Matra Vey
The cyberwitch of the Witchcoil Spire. She believes the city must be stitched into one thinking body so no child will ever again be abandoned by chance, hunger, or civic neglect. She is not random evil; she is a totalizing caretaker whose solution abolishes consent.

## Full Narrative Structure

### Prologue: The Hushfall
Bango attends the Nightcomb Feast in Brassroot Borough with Tula and Patoot. The district is introduced through warm hive lanterns, improvised brass altars, worker songs, and unstable municipal lighting. Minor exploration establishes Bango's motion basics and Patoot's scout prompts. The feast is interrupted by an engineered blackout pulse. Distorted hymn-audio rolls through the borough, bees become disoriented, and masked raiders descend through maintenance grates. Tula pushes Bango clear during the attack and is taken in a cage-lift toward the lower transit line. Bango reaches the first apiary shrine too late to stop the abduction, but receives Tula's first echo-memory: the kidnappers serve a witch above and below the city at once.

### Act I: Brassroot Borough
Objective: restore the first shrine circuit, learn the economy, find the raiders' route.

Bango and Patoot track hive thieves through worker tenements, steam ducts, rooftop laundries, and abandoned apiaries. The player learns the first major loops: Hive Sigils, Nocturne Notes, Lantern Kin rescues, and shrine refinement. Vilm Siltcoat reveals that several factions bought the same blackout maps. The act ends with Bango defeating the Honey Debt Collector, a gang lieutenant who has been melting shrine parts into weapons. A transit seal opens, exposing Saint Voltage Arcade.

### Act II: Saint Voltage Arcade
Objective: infiltrate miracle markets and chapel-gang circuits.

Saint Voltage is a neon lit chapel-market where indulgence machines, arcade cabinets, votive kiosks, and street gangs overlap. Tula's second echo reveals that Matra Vey is building a memory loom fed by devotion and urban signal traffic. Bango encounters Sister Halceon and discovers that old cathedral wiring was repurposed into obedience relays. The act's boss, Bishop Static, is a former preacher fused into a slot-machine choir pulpit. When defeated, he drops the Choir Key and exposes the rail routes leading to Ossuary Transit.

### Act III: Ossuary Transit
Objective: reopen the rail backbone and uncover the city's hidden dead routes.

Broken trains, bone furnaces, memorial vaults, and collapsed stations define this act. Here the player learns that the city historically buried unwanted communities inside unused infrastructure. Matra Vey has been harvesting these forgotten layers as both literal material and symbolic fuel. Tula's third echo confirms she is being used as a stable anchor for the ritual engine because her chant-memory can hold patterns without corruption. The act ends with the defeat of Railmouth Ossivar, an ossified signal-engine beast whose bones ring like switchyard bells.

### Act IV: Gutterwake Sewers
Objective: interrupt the alchemical runoff that feeds the ritual engine.

Gutterwake is humid, fungal, and alive with floodgates, drowned workshops, and cryptid nests. Patoot becomes especially important here because aerial scouting reveals water patterns and ambushes. Bango learns that runoff from industrial miracle works is being filtered into shrine wells to sour the city's sacred sites. The boss, Maw-of-Silt, is a blind sewer cryptid grown huge on chemical honey. Purifying the sewer sluices weakens Matra Vey's inbetween power and restores the second half of the shrine fast-travel lattice.

### Act V: Cinder Tenements
Objective: free captive households and break the apartment chapel network.

This district is the emotional center of the game. Ordinary homes contain hidden altars, confession vents, and stolen child-objects mounted like relics. The player rescues multiple Lantern Kin here and sees the human cost of the cult. Tula's fourth echo reveals she has begun sabotaging Matra Vey's resonance timings from within the Spire. The district boss is the Caretaker Red Mother, a cult matron who uses nursery machinery, hymn cradles, and soot dolls. After her defeat, the city above briefly flickers awake, showing just how wide the crisis has become.

### Act VI: Aviary of the Last Broadcast
Objective: seize control of the city's one intact mass signal tower.

The Aviary is made of signal masts, giant cages, old speaker arrays, and public-address chambers. Here the game shifts into large vertical traversal. Patoot's glide assist, cable perches, and wind readouts are central. Bango learns that Matra Vey plans to broadcast a binding hymn through every speaker in the city at once. The act's boss, the Broadcast Harrower, is a stitched flock-machine that emits sonic patterns forcing enemies into frenzy states. Defeating it lets Bango upload Tula's counter-hymn to the city's last clean tower.

### Act VII: The Witchcoil Spire
Objective: ascend the ritual-factory, rescue Tula, and rupture the city-body engine.

The Spire combines all previous motifs: chapel steel, hive comb geometry, machine ribs, rail logic, organ pipes, and vertical sacrifice lifts. Matra Vey reveals her thesis openly: cities already consume children, memory, labor, and faith; she only chose to centralize the process into something honest. Tula is recovered mid-act, but weak. She and Patoot combine to keep the counter-hymn stable while Bango ascends into the final chamber. The first phase of the final boss is Matra Vey as a ritual engineer using choir drones, memory spears, and rotating shrine fragments. The second phase fuses her with the city's obedience lattice. The win condition is not only depleting health but also striking exposed harmony anchors in the correct sensory order using patterns established throughout the game.

### Ending
The ritual engine collapses. Not every district heals instantly. Some factions become more violent in the aftermath, others freer. Tula survives, but the hive-memory network is permanently changed. The closing image is not a neat restoration. Instead, the city finally becomes legible as a place that can be tended instead of merely endured.

### Postgame
The postgame unlocks sealed challenge shrines, remixed district states, faction-resolution quests, and an optional true ending route in which Bango repairs the Last Broadcast lattice into a voluntary civic memory archive.

## World Structure

### Hub
The central hub is Apiary Junction, a layered undercity plaza connected to all seven major districts. It contains the principal shrine, move practice chambers, Vilm's salvage stall, Tula's memory room after rescue, and faction bulletin niches.

### District Progression Order
1. Brassroot Borough
2. Saint Voltage Arcade
3. Ossuary Transit
4. Gutterwake Sewers
5. Cinder Tenements
6. Aviary of the Last Broadcast
7. Witchcoil Spire

### District Logic Rules
- Each district contains 1 major shrine, 2 minor rest shrines, 1 boss arena, 3 to 5 landmark traversal puzzles, 2 rescue chains, and one faction resolution side route.
- Each district has a readable macro loop visible from early vantage points.
- Every district changes visually after shrine purification.
- Every district gains or loses hostile density based on player faction handling.

## Gameplay Systems

### Traversal
Bango's base traversal kit includes run, jump, ledge grab, push, pull, slam, horn bash, and short sprint. Patoot expands this with hover assist, burst glide stabilization, perch scouting, switch pecking, thread line carries, and midair correction. Upgrades unlock wall-runs, honey rail grinding, drop-hover braking, shrine pull grapples, and combat traversal cancels.

### Collectibles
- Hive Sigils: primary macro progression items. Required for district access and shrine lattice repair.
- Nocturne Notes: common collectible currency used for purchases, upgrades, and optional codex unlocks.
- Lantern Kin: rescue set pieces that unlock passive social and shrine bonuses.
- Shrine Pollen: secondary crafting component used to reinforce abilities.
- Choir Scraps: lore fragments tied to enemy doctrines and hidden ritual routes.

### Attributes
- Vigor: maximum health and stagger resistance.
- Honeyheart: shrine efficiency, healing output, and pollen recovery.
- Taloncraft: projectile skill, trap utility, and Patoot damage scaling.
- Nerve: stamina pool and fear resistance.
- Grit: defense, shove power, and environmental damage reduction.
- Arcana: ward power, ritual interactions, and spell damage.

### Attribute Gain Logic
- Every full Hive Sigil grants 1 stat point.
- Every purified district shrine grants 1 bonus catalyst point.
- Lantern Kin rescue thresholds grant specialization modifiers instead of raw points.

### Stamina and Recovery Logic
- Base stamina is 100.
- Light actions cost 8 to 14.
- Heavy actions cost 20 to 35.
- Dodges cost 18.
- Aerial corrections cost 10.
- Stamina regen begins 0.65 seconds after the last stamina-spending action.
- Nerve increases max stamina by 6 points per attribute rank.

### Combat
Combat is close-quarters and positional, not crowd-musou. Bango uses claws, horns, body checks, and shrine wards. Patoot adds pressure, interrupts, and short-range support shots. Combat actions derive from stance, movement state, and unlocked skill tags rather than a tiny fixed move list.

### Move Catalog Logic
The long-term target is a 1000-slot move catalog, but implementation begins with procedural naming and authored key families.

Each move is composed from:
- stance
- locomotion mode
- body emphasis
- angle
- element or ward modifier
- combo index
- utility tags
- fear-state modifier

Each move stores:
- stamina cost
- hit count
- damage scalar
- knockback scalar
- elemental or ward tag
- keyframe count
- interpolation hint profile
- camera push profile
- contact sound profile

### Skill Trees
- Horn Path: guard breaks, charge bashes, crowd shoves.
- Claw Path: combo length, bleed-style wound states, rapid cancel options.
- Patoot Path: scout reveal range, peck interrupts, spit wards, rescue assist.
- Shrine Path: better refinement returns, longer ward uptime, safer rest states.
- Salvage Path: lock cracking, hazard disarming, secret route opening.
- Resonance Path: faction handling, memory tuning, social outcomes.

### Shrines
Shrines are the game's main structural device.

Shrines allow:
- healing and checkpointing
- attribute spending
- skill unlocking
- fast travel between purified nodes
- faction response updates
- day-state and hazard-state advancement

Shrine refinement converts collected honey and shrine pollen into catalysts. Resting at a shrine may also repopulate enemies or change district social states.

### Quest Logic
- Main quest tracks district completion and Tula echo recovery.
- Rescue quests track Lantern Kin chains.
- Faction quests branch between shrine keepers, salvagers, chapel defectors, and borough gangs.
- Optional myth quests unlock hidden bosses and codex entries.

### EgoSphere Relationship Logic
NPEs track trust, fear, debt, respect, rivalry, and mimicry. These values affect:
- vendor pricing
- whether bystanders warn the player of ambushes
- whether gangs join cult pressure events
- enemy pursuit persistence
- which rescue routes become available

### Difficulty Model
Difficulty emerges from density and systemic layering, not just enemy health inflation. Each district has three state bands:
- calm
- hostile
- crisis

State bands influence patrol count, ambush scripting, environmental hazards, and support NPC availability.

### Failure States
- Player death respawns at the last activated shrine.
- Currency loss is partial, not total.
- Unspent pollen drops as reclaimable residue.
- Some story events lock until the player stabilizes a district.

### Save Structure
Save data stores:
- district completion
- shrine states
- collectibles found
- attribute and skill allocations
- move unlock flags
- relationship matrices
- quest flags
- current inventory and currencies

## Controls and UI

### Top Screen
- stereoscopic world view
- health, stamina, ward, and current objective
- lock-on marker and contextual traversal prompt

### Bottom Screen
- area map
- shrine menu
- skill and move pages
- party relationship summaries
- objective and collectible counts

## Audio Direction
The score blends ritual percussion, industrial drone, broken choir, reed instruments, and hive-like harmonic buzzing. Districts should each have a tonal color identity. Bango sounds heavy but not clumsy. Patoot sounds sharp, alert, and emotionally readable. Shrine audio should sound restorative without becoming soft or pastoral.

## Art Direction
The visual direction is horned-prehistoric anatomy meeting soot-blackened civic infrastructure, brass devotional machinery, old urban folklore, and hive geometry. Colors should favor ochre, rust, dead teal, soot black, lantern amber, acidic green, and shrine gold. The project should look tactile, layered, and slightly unhealthy even when safe.
