V. COMPLETE GAME NARRATIVE
===========================

NARRATIVE FRAME -- The Subliminal Spine
----------------------------------------
Every piece of named content in SKAZKA: Terranova is the story of a boy who keeps watching
the same video on the internet. He doesn't know this. The player, accumulating small textual
anomalies, eventually does.


ACT I -- THE ROOM AND THE WOOD
--------------------------------

Scene 1 -- Misha's Room (morning)

[Narrator VO -- VO_001]
"You've been here before. You just don't remember it yet."

[Narrator VO -- VO_002]
"His name is Misha. The cassette player is already warm. Someone pressed play before he woke up."

Misha stands in his room. The player can examine three objects before the door is accessible:

  - The Cassette Player -> [VO_003]: "It plays a song he hasn't heard. He hums along anyway."
  - The Window -> [VO_004]: "The village. Birch and smoke. A crow on the fence that does not move when called."
  - The Folk Doll on the shelf -> [VO_005]: "She's been turned to face the wall again. He doesn't remember doing that."

When the player moves to exit:

[Narrator VO -- VO_006]
"The door was locked yesterday. Today it isn't. He decides not to think about why."

------------------------------------------------------------
MEGABOSS: KOSCHEI-DEATHLESS / TANK-PRIME
"The Deathless Engine -- He Who Runs the Loop"
------------------------------------------------------------

PHASE 1 -- Koschei-Tank Prime (at full HP)
Visual: Giant skeletal figure fused with Newgrounds Tank chassis. Cassette tape spinal column.
Folk rune sigils embedded in armour plates like circuit boards. Moves with bureaucratic deliberateness.

[MegaBoss Phase 1 intro -- VO_053]
"Log entry. Loop seventeen. Irregular player detected. Additional loop has been -- felt appropriate
to say: I am tired. But I am not permitted to stop. Are you?"

Phase 1 attacks:
  - Beat Cannon: Fires a slow projectile on every 4th beat -- perfectly dodgeable if player is in-beat.
  - Rune Sweep: Arm sweep telegraphed 2 beats early -- parry window.
  - Folder Drop: Summons 3 stacks of administrative folders -- destroy via ritual or they stack into damage pillars.
  - Loop Echo: At 66% HP -- spawns a near-transparent copy of Misha (half-power). Echo says VO_046 on spawn.

QTE Event 1 (Phase 1 to 2 transition at 40% HP):
  Screen freezes on Koschei staggering. QTE window opens.
  [VO_054]: "He reaches inside the chassis. Something is being removed."
  Prompt sequence: [A] [X] [RT] [LT] -- represents "pulling the tape from the drive"
  SUCCESS: Tape flies out, phase 2 begins with explosion FX.
  FAIL: Koschei heals 10% HP. QTE does not repeat -- combat continues.

---

PHASE 2 -- Koschei Unshelled (40%-15% HP)
Visual: Tank chassis shattered. Exposed skeleton drive-core. Runes moving across bones.
Orange aura. Faster, more erratic.

[Phase 2 dialogue -- VO_055]
"There. Now you see it. The drive. The deathless part. You can't destroy it. It's backed up
in the tape you're carrying."

[Narrator VO -- VO_056]
"...Misha checks his pocket. The tape from his room. It is warm."

Phase 2 attacks:
  - Bone Chain: Long-range chain attack -- requires beat-timed double parry.
  - Rune Barrage: Three rune-missiles in irregular rhythm -- breaks player's beat sync if hit.
  - Admin Override: Summons Zmeya Officer shade -- defeat in 8 beats or Koschei heals 5%.
  - Temporal Fold: At 25% HP -- screen briefly (1 frame) shows Misha's room background. (Full subliminal moment.)

QTE Event 2 (Phase 2 to 3 transition at 15% HP):
  [VO_057]: "He opens his chest. There is a cassette player inside. It is playing your tape."
  Prompt: [X] [A] [A] [LT] [RT] -- "press play on the override track"
  SUCCESS: Phase 3 begins.
  FAIL: Phase 2 resets to 25% HP. QTE available again at 15%.

---

PHASE 3 -- The Name (15%-0%)
Visual: All visuals strip back to silhouette. Screen goes to near-black. BGM: heartbeat sub-bass +
one sustained note. Koschei visible only as negative-space cutout with self-assembling runes.

[Phase 3 dialogue -- VO_058]

VII. NEWGROUNDS x SLAVIC FOLKLORE ICONOGRAPHY FUSION DESIGN SYSTEM
====================================================================

VISUAL GRAMMAR
---------------
- Colour palette: Muted Slavic earthtones (ochre, birch-white, pine-shadow green, deep red-oxide)
  corrupted by Newgrounds orange (#FF6600) on broken/corrupted elements. Orange = the intrusion.

- Border language: All UI panels use folk-embroidery micro-borders. Where embroidery is interrupted
  by a corrupted element, it resolves into pixelated Newgrounds logo fragments.

- Enemy design rule: Every enemy has one Newgrounds-culture reference fused onto a Slavic archetype.
  The Zmeya Officer wears the Tank logo as a badge. The Rusalka's hair floats in a looping animated
  GIF frame. The Poludnitsa's scythe is a film-scrub slider.

- Text rule: Lore text is set in Leshy Script. All "corrupted" or "from the other world" text appears
  in Terranova Sans with random characters replaced by Newgrounds mascot ASCII glyphs.

- Sound rule: Every BGM track uses traditional Slavic instrumentation as the melodic layer. The rhythmic
  layer is lo-fi electronic -- specifically sounds recognisable from 2000s Flash era (GarageBand
  presets, 8-bit SFX used as percussion).

- Architecture rule: All Slavic structures are proportionally correct to folk references. Each has one
  panel or sign that is clearly a rendered webpage -- a 404 error, a broken image placeholder, a
  "skip intro" button carved in stone.


VIII. SUBLIMINAL SUBTEXT SYSTEM
=================================

IMPLEMENTATION
---------------
At precisely defined moments, one frame (or 2 frames at 60fps) of a SubtextFlash image (FX_010)
is inserted into the rendering pipeline. These are invisible to conscious perception at normal
playback speed but visible if the player captures a screenshot at the right millisecond.
The SubtextFlash images form a message when read in order.

SUBTEXT EVENTS (in order)
--------------------------
#    Trigger                         SubtextFlash content
1    Player first enters Dark Wood   A simple counter: "1" in Leshy Script
2    Rusalka defeated                "2"
3    Poludnitsa defeated             "3"
4    Bone Market first entered       "4"
5    Zmeya Officer defeated          "5"
6    Cassette Ritual completed       "6"
7    Veil Threshold entered          "7"
8    MegaBoss Phase 1 to 2 QTE       "8"
9    MegaBoss Phase 2 to 3 QTE       "9"
10   Final QTE round 4 success       "10"
11   Credits roll begins             Misha's room. Empty. Cassette player on shelf. Counter reads "18."

Additionally, SFX_024 (ChildLaughter_Distant) is mixed at -18dB under BGM_005 (Dark Wood) and
BGM_008 (Veil Threshold). Inaudible on phone speakers. Audible on headphones at specific loudness level.
