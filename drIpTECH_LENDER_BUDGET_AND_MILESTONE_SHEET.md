# drIpTECH Lender Budget and Milestone Sheet

## Purpose

This sheet translates the portfolio plan and NanoPlay_t strategy into a lender-facing summary of intended fund use, milestones, and risk controls.

This is a planning document, not legal, accounting, or lending advice.

## Funding Objective

Requested financing range:

- lower case: CAD 120,000
- operating target: CAD 220,000 to CAD 280,000
- defensive upper band: CAD 350,000

Recommended working ask for planning:

- CAD 260,000

## Use of Funds at CAD 260,000

### Personal and legacy liabilities to isolate

- CRA overdue amount: CAD 14,000
- additional extended-credit or disputed debt reserve: CAD 10,000

Planning treatment:
- ring-fence these amounts from product operating capital
- document them separately in lender discussions so business execution capacity can be evaluated clearly

### Business and product uses

- incorporation, accounting, legal setup, lender compliance, filing reserve: CAD 18,000
- hardware engineering and pilot industrialization: CAD 22,000
- first 1,000-unit NanoPlay_t prototype batch reserve: CAD 118,000
- software and game development reserve: CAD 26,000
- QA, test fixtures, replacement parts, and failure analysis: CAD 9,000
- certification and import/export documentation reserve: CAD 8,000
- marketing and demo capture reserve: CAD 12,000
- contingency reserve: CAD 23,000

Total planned allocation:
- CAD 260,000

## Business Thesis

### Revenue path

Primary near-term revenue path:
- software-first launch through PlayHub / DripDungeons-style products

Secondary premium experimentation path:
- limited NanoPlay_t hardware pilot built on off-the-shelf Linux handheld architecture

Reasoning:
- software validates demand and reduces capital risk
- hardware remains a constrained premium pilot rather than the first full-scale business dependency

## Risk Controls

### Financial controls

- separate personal debt cleanup from product operating cash in records and reporting
- maintain a contingency reserve throughout the project
- treat hardware as capped-risk capital, not the sole growth engine

### Technical controls

- require smoke tests and repeatable builds for core stack projects
- use off-the-shelf hardware architecture for NanoPlay_t
- avoid custom silicon, bespoke OS forks, and high-overhead online infrastructure in the first financing cycle

### Commercial controls

- use PlayHub software releases to validate demand before scaling hardware
- preserve the ability to pause hardware expansion if demand or margin assumptions weaken

## Lender Narrative

The recommended lender-facing narrative is:

- drIpTECH is consolidating a broad prototype workspace into one coherent software and hardware-adjacent stack
- the immediate commercial path is software-first
- the hardware program is a disciplined pilot, not an uncontrolled platform bet
- financing is being requested to complete business formation, isolate legacy liabilities, stabilize the core stack, deliver a vertical slice, and prepare a constrained premium hardware pilot

## Bottom Line

The defensible financing position is:

- request approximately CAD 260,000
- isolate CAD 24,000 of that for debt and tax cleanup planning reserves
- direct the remaining capital into formation, core stack stabilization, vertical slice delivery, and a capped hardware pilot path
- keep expansion tied to measurable software traction and prototype execution milestones
