This is the first full design consolidation for the project.

High Concept
Crepulin: Enfant; .xxDetnDimension is a 2.5D horror action-adventure about a child navigating a parasitic mirror-layer hidden inside an aging apartment district. The player character, Enfant, slips into the DetnDimension, a folded city-space made from household superstition, abandoned civic infrastructure, and predatory memory residue called Crepulin.

Core Pillars
1. Child-scale horror through familiar spaces made wrong.
2. Folklore-infused systems and ritual rules.
3. Stealth, pursuit, and intimate survival.
4. Environmental storytelling through civic interiors and utility spaces.
5. Strong forward-facing sprite identity and occult-industrial sound.

Product Definition
- Genre: 2.5D horror action-adventure with light metroidvania structure.
- Camera: side-on exploration with frontal confrontation frames.
- Target playtime: 8 to 12 hours main path.

Why the Project Looks This Way
The two existing sprite files strongly imply an early forward-facing presentation for the protagonist. The public handle traces suggest an adjacent aesthetic shaped by underground beat culture, lo-fi intensity, abstract naming, and industrial mood.

World Premise
Crepulin is the dusk-film that gathers in neglected living spaces where fear, silence, and old rituals overlap. When enough Crepulin accumulates, the DetnDimension appears: a child-scaled mirror-city built from corridors, kitchens, elevators, pipes, yards, stations, and burial cavities that have absorbed generations of warnings told to keep children alive.

Main Characters
Enfant:
The playable child protagonist. Observant, quiet, brave in bursts, and able to learn ritual rules faster than adults.

Crepulin:
A memory-reactive residue and distributed antagonist that stores fear, distorts architecture, and enforces legend logic.

The Domov Keeper:
A household spirit derived from Domovoy belief. Helpful when respected, punishing when angered.

The Keyhole Kikimora:
A recurring nightmare stalker entering through cracks, keyholes, curtains, and vents.

Babka Kuroles:
An original threshold guide inspired by Baba Yaga, living in a mobile utility hut.

The Chauffeur of the Black Road:
Recurring pursuer inspired by Black Volga legends. Travels through service roads, walls, and underground routes.

The Bird Over Reactor Nine:
An omen creature appearing before major collapse events.

Story Summary
Enfant lives in Block Nine, a decaying apartment district. After a blackout and an elevator failure, a child disappears. Enfant follows the traces into maintenance corridors and slips into the DetnDimension, where legends are physically real and every room has rules.

Narrative Structure
Prologue: The Courtyard Warning
Children swap rumors about the Black Car, the curtain room, the keyhole woman, and the hole that speaks. A blackout begins the first disappearance.

Act I: Stairwell Mouth
Explore stairwells, fuse closets, pipe shafts, and utility corridors. Learn the House Rule system and encounter the first Black Road sighting.

Act II: Curtain Flats
Apartment interiors warped by haunted curtain myths. The Keyhole Kikimora becomes an active stalker.

Act III: Bathhouse Veins
Communal bathhouses and steam passages. Babka Kuroles introduces barter-based myth logic and Warmth.

Act IV: Railbone Yards
Dead tracks and signal sheds reveal that the Black Road is a transit principle, not only a vehicle route.

Act V: Well Chamber
Descending into drilling rooms and echo wells reveals buried civic catastrophe and accumulated denial.

Act VI: The Black Road Procession
The final run uses everything learned from domestic rites, spirit bargains, and refusal rules to confront the Chauffeur.

Ending
Some missing children return. Adults finally speak openly about what they ignored. Some rooms become safe again. Crepulin recedes but does not vanish.

Core Mechanics
Exploration:
Climb, crouch, crawl, ledge traverse, and use child-sized shortcuts.

Hiding:
Use beds, tables, curtains, carts, and alcoves. Not all threats can be avoided by hiding in the same way.

Listening:
Listen to walls, doors, floors, and vents to learn routes, hidden spaces, and legend-state conditions.

House Rule System:
Rooms have embedded rules such as not leaving bread on the floor, not facing mirrors while sleeping, and not answering corridor voices at the wrong hour.

Dread and Warmth:
Dread rises from pursuit, corrupted spaces, and forbidden sound. Warmth comes from steam, stoves, candles, and spirit blessing. High Dread distorts perception and control.

Visual Direction
Forward-facing child sprite identity, damp concrete, old plaster, sodium light, steam white, black lacquer vehicle shine, chalk marks, and bruised shadow color.

Audio Direction
Apartment ambience, industrial drone, lo-fi beat textures, tape hiss, low choir, rail resonance, and distorted child counting. The sound should feel touched by underground beatmaking culture rather than generic orchestral horror.

Interface Direction
Menus and overlays should feel like maintenance diagrams, prayer cards, utility labels, elevator notes, and child chalk marks.
