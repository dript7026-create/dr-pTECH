# Gravo Market Design Brief

Objective:
Build the most commercially viable contemporary Gravo game possible within the existing ORBEngine direction, while keeping a distinct identity instead of chasing a generic Soulslike clone.

Market position:
- Premium PS5 action-horror with readable combat, dense atmosphere, strong visual identity, and persistent rival ecology.
- Short-to-mid session viability with shrine-based return loops and high replay value through adaptive enemies.
- Distinct selling point: ORBEngine pseudo-3D surreal traversal plus MindSphereRivalary-driven enemy memory.

Core pillars:
- Real-time combat with melee/ranged interplay.
- Exploration-first neighborhood horror rather than corridor-only combat gauntlets.
- Bossless structure replaced by recurring high-threat rivals and biome-scale escalation.
- Mirror shrines as emotional reset points, build respec points, and narrative checkpoints.
- Persistent enemies that remember outcomes, rise in status, and adapt their spacing and aggression.

Combat target shape:
- Faster than classic Soulslike stamina drag, slower than character-action spectacle.
- Clear commitment windows, readable enemy silhouette language, and strong spacing discipline.
- Beamshot Eye handles interrupt, spacing, marking, and exposure.
- Beamsword handles burst, poise break, and close-quarters control.
- High-tier enemies should mix pressure, evasive responses, and retaliation, not sponge HP.

Progression target shape:
- Shrine-to-shrine traversal loop.
- Narrative beats tied to places, not only cutscenes.
- Build growth through restraint, insight, beam modulation, recovery tuning, and rival knowledge.
- Enemy promotions and grudges should alter future encounters.

Content structure target:
- 1 hero sheet family for Gravo across stance, traversal, hit, shrine, and expression sets.
- 4 to 6 basic enemy families.
- 4 to 6 high-tier recurring rival families.
- 6 to 8 major biomes, each with multiple angles and traversal silhouettes.
- Modular props, decals, shrine variants, FX atlases, and HUD layers.

Visual target shape:
- Transparent-background production assets.
- Strong silhouette readability first, detail second.
- Lighting, shade, highlight, and curvature should imply pseudo-3D volume usable by ORBSystems.
- Concept art must expose attachment logic, depth cues, and clean negative space for extraction and rerendering.
